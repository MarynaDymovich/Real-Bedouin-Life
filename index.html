HISTORY OF WADI RUM

Wadi Rum, the Valley of the Moon, first chronicled by explorers in the early 19th century. This stunning desert landscape boasts towering sandstone cliffs and ancient petroglyphs from Nabatean and Thamudic tribes, marking it as a significant crossroads of ancient civilizations. Follow in the footsteps of Lawrence of Arabia and traverse the red sands by camel or 4x4. As the sun sets, enjoy a night under the stars at a traditional Bedouin camp, savoring authentic cuisine. Wadi Rum is more than a destination—it's a journey through history and adventure. Book your tour now and experience the timeless allure of this desert wonder!

FAQ

How do I get to Wadi Rum?
To get to Wadi Rum, you can drive approximately 4 hours from Amman or 1 hour from Aqaba to Wadi Rum village. there is a bus service to Wadi Rum village, but please note that buses are quite rare, typically running once a day. Once you arrive at the village, I will personally pick you up in my jeep and take you on the tour. At the end of the tour, I'll ensure you're safely transported back to the village.
How can I get to Wadi Rum by bus?
Buses to Wadi Rum are often operated by the JETT (Jordan Express Tourist Transportation) company from the South Bus Station (Mujamma Janoub) in Amman. JETT is a reliable operator that provides scheduled services to various destinations across Jordan, including Wadi Rum. These buses usually run once a day. It's advisable to check the current schedule and availability beforehand, as they can be infrequent. Once you arrive at Wadi Rum village, I'll be there to pick you up in my jeep for your desert tour.
What should I bring for a visit to Wadi Rum?
Essentials include comfortable clothing, sturdy shoes, a hat, sunscreen, sunglasses, and a camera. For overnight stays, bring warm clothes as desert temperatures can drop at night.
When is the best time to visit Wadi Rum?
You can visit Wadi Rum throughout the year, as its beauty shines in every season. During spring (March to May) and autumn (September to November) temperatures are mild and ideal for exploring the desert. However, Wadi Rum's beauty can be appreciated in any season, each offering its own unique charm and experience.
What activities can I do in Wadi Rum?
You can enjoy jeep tours, camel rides, hiking, rock climbing, hot air balloon rides, and stargazing. There are also opportunities to experience Bedouin culture and stay in traditional desert camps.
What is included in a Wadi Rum desert tour?
A typical Wadi Rum desert tour includes transportation to and from Wadi Rum, guided jeep tour to explore the desert, visits to iconic sites like Lawrence's Spring and ancient rock formations, meals (depending on the tour package), and optional activities such as camel rides or overnight stays in Bedouin camps.
Is it safe to visit Wadi Rum on a tour?
Yes, Wadi Rum is a very safe destination for tourists. Local guides are knowledgeable about the area and will ensure your safety during tours. It's important to follow their instructions and respect the natural environment.
How to pay in Wadi Rum?
To make payments in Wadi Rum, please ensure you have Jordan Dinars (JD) in cash as there are no ATM or credit card facilities available in the desert.
